# AETHERSONG — The First Refrain
## World Bible

> An original kid-appropriate JRPG, inspired by the genre. No Final Fantasy IP — invented world, cast, lore, names.

---

## 1. Premise (the elevator pitch)

The world of **Saevora** was sung into being by the **First Choir** — seven divine voices whose harmony anchors reality itself. A thousand years ago the final note of their refrain was lost. Ever since, the world has been quietly forgetting itself.

Where the song dies, the world unmakes into **the Hush**: silver-grey patches of silence that erase land, memory, and people. Music here is literal magic — every spell is a verse, every weapon hums with a refrain, every healer sings.

A teenage girl with an off-key voice and an old lantern is the only person the spreading Hush flinches from. She doesn''t know why. She is about to find out.

---

## 2. The World

**Continent: Saevora.** Three regions matter for the story:

- **The Coastlands of Threnfall** (start) — fishing villages, salt cliffs, a half-forgotten chapel up the hill. Always overcast. The first Hush patches appeared here.
- **The Cathedral City of Vellanthe** (Ch. 2) — bell towers, choir-academies, the Vault of Verses. Politics. Beauty.
- **The Verdigris** (Ch. 3) — green woodlands where trees remember what people forget. Flute-singers tend them.
- **The Harbor of Brassmouth** (Ch. 4) — multicultural port. Half-drowned ruins beneath the waves hold the first Verse.

**Rules of magic — the Song.** Everyone in Saevora has a *voice-tone*: the unique resonance of their soul. Pure tones can shape the world. Singing a learned **Verse** triggers a spell — fire, healing, illusion. The clearer the voice, the stronger the verse. Liora is "flat" — her tone hits no clean note — and yet the Hush flinches from her, because the Hush is *too perfect*, and her flatness is a key it cannot lock.

**The Hush.** Not death, not destruction — *unmaking*. A Hushed village does not burn; it grows quiet. The houses dim. People forget what they were doing, then who they were, then they are gone, and nobody remembers they existed except those carrying a Hush-touched object (like Liora''s lantern).

## 3. The Cast

### Liora Vey — Lantern-bearer (protagonist)
- **Age:** 17. **Role:** warrior/protagonist. **Weapon:** her mother''s shortsword and the silver lantern.
- **Origin:** Threnfall, a coastal village. Daughter of Marin Vey, a Hushwalker (someone who marks the boundaries of Hush patches so travelers don''t wander in). Mother went missing six months before the game starts.
- **Personality:** Stubborn, dry-humored, deeply scared but very good at hiding it. Believes she is unmusical and ordinary. Wrong on both counts.
- **The hook:** She cannot sing a Verse. Her voice is flat. But when she sings *anything*, the Hush *flinches*. She is the first person in a thousand years whose tone the Unisonist cannot absorb.
- **Arc:** From "I am unremarkable" to "I am the only one who can stand here."

### Toren Ash — Choir-apprentice (mage, joins Ch. 2)
- **Age:** 19. **Role:** mage/scholar. **Verses:** Ember, Sparkflare, Bookwind.
- **Origin:** Vellanthe Cathedral. Was being trained to be a Voice (Choir initiate) until he asked the wrong question — "If the Refrain was lost, who lost it?" — and was demoted. Now a runaway.
- **Personality:** Kind, brilliant, vain, prone to lecturing. Genuinely faithful to the *song* but skeptical of the *institution* that claims to keep it. Wants to believe.
- **Arc:** From "the Choir is corrupt" to "the song is bigger than the choir."

### Sira Mosswind — Forest-singer (healer/druid, joins Ch. 3)
- **Age:** 22. **Role:** healer/support. **Verses:** Mossbind, Greenwake, Quiet Garden.
- **Origin:** The Verdigris, the green forest country. A flute-singer; channels the world-melody through hand-carved wooden flutes made from "remembering trees" (trees that record what passes near them).
- **Personality:** Cynical about the Choir, devoted to the song. Adores children, distrusts adults. Hides her grief under a sharp tongue. Was once married — her wife was Hushed and even Sira cannot remember her face anymore.
- **Arc:** From "I have already lost the most important thing" to "what is left is also worth saving."

### Pip "Bell" Korren — Harbor pickpocket (rogue, joins Ch. 4)
- **Age:** 14. **Role:** rogue/mimic. **Verses:** Misdirection, Borrowed Voice, Bell Toll.
- **Origin:** Brassmouth, the harbor port. Orphan. Can mimic any voice she hears with eerie precision — useful for stealing spells.
- **Personality:** Cocky, loyal, brittle. Adopts Liora as a big sister within an hour of meeting her, which makes Liora deeply uncomfortable in a way she pretends to hate.
- **Arc:** From "I do not need anyone" to "I would rather have these people than the world I knew."

## 4. The Antagonist

### The Unisonist — Eighth Voice (the singer cast out)
- **True name:** Vaeran. Once the eighth member of what was meant to be an eight-voice Choir.
- **Origin:** Cast out of the First Choir for proposing that the world be *perfected* — every voice tuned to a single note, every life simplified to a single tone, all dissonance erased.
- **Belief:** Free will is dissonance. Dissonance is what is killing the song. If every voice in Saevora joins his perfect chord, the world will be saved — and made beautiful.
- **Tactic:** The Hush is not a weapon. It is *his ongoing harmonization* spreading outward. To him, the people inside Hushed villages are not dead — they are joined. They are inside him, singing one note, finally at peace.
- **Why he is not just a villain:** He genuinely thinks he is saving the world. He is *partially right*: the song is dying. He just has a monstrous answer.
- **Why Liora terrifies him:** Her flat voice cannot be absorbed. She is dissonance the Unisonist cannot resolve. He needs her *converted*, not killed. So he is patient. He hunts gently.

---

## 5. Central Conflict

The First Refrain was broken into **seven Verses**, one per Voice, scattered across Saevora before the First Choir vanished. The Unisonist is hunting them too. To restore the world, the party must find all seven and sing them in correct sequence at the **Vault of Verses** in Vellanthe.

But there is a catch. The Refrain needs an **eighth voice** to anchor the chord. Liora''s flat voice can serve — but the song would forever be slightly off-key, the world forever imperfect. The Unisonist offers a "perfect" alternative: he will sing the eighth note, the chord will be flawless, and dissonance — choice, individuality, grief — will be gently sung out of the world.

**Liora''s choice at the climax:** Save the world with a permanently imperfect song, or hand it to the Unisonist to be made beautiful and small.

She chooses imperfection.

## 6. Chapter Outline (7 chapters)

1. **The Lantern at the Hush** *(Phase 1 vertical slice)* — Threnfall village. Liora''s mother''s lantern is found in the chapel up the hill. The chapel has been touched by the Hush; something is inside. Liora goes in alone. She finds the lantern, fights through Hush-echoes, beats the Silent Chapel boss (a wraith called the Hush Echo), and leaves the village with the lantern lit. *End of slice.*

2. **The Lost Apprentice** — Liora reaches Vellanthe. The Choir denies the Hush exists. She meets Toren hiding behind the Vault of Verses. They flee together when Choir-guards come for him.

3. **The Forest Sings Back** — In the Verdigris, Sira teaches Liora that the silver lantern is one of seven — the **Hushlamps**, made by the First Choir to mark where the Refrain''s pieces fell. They find Verse I in a sunken grove.

4. **The Bell in the Harbor** — Brassmouth is half-Hushed. Pip joins after they pull her out of a Hushed alley. Verse II is in the undersea ruins of a drowned cathedral.

5. **The Unisonist Speaks** — Confrontation. The party meets Vaeran. He does not fight them. He sings, gently, and three of them feel a *pull*. Only Liora is untouched. He leaves, knowing he must convert her, not crush her. The party realizes the scale of what they are fighting.

6. **The Seven Verses** — Verses III–VII across the continent. Side stories. Recurring boss: **Hush Resonants** — fragments of the Unisonist''s harmony given form. (This is the body of the game.)

7. **The Last Refrain** — Return to Vellanthe. The Vault of Verses. The Unisonist makes his final offer. Liora chooses to sing the Refrain with her flat voice. The world is saved, imperfectly. The Unisonist is not destroyed — he is *out-sung*, and walks away alone into a quieter horizon. The world remains messy. The party goes home. Final shot: Liora sings, off-key, at her mother''s grave. Her mother''s lantern glows.

## 7. Tone & Voice

- **Kid-appropriate but not saccharine.** Loss is real (Liora''s mother is missing; Sira''s wife is gone; villages have been erased). It is handled with restraint — never gore, never cruelty for its own sake.
- **Heartfelt.** The story is about choosing the messy, imperfect, real over the smooth, perfect, dead.
- **Hopeful, ultimately.** The Refrain can be sung, badly, by a flat-voiced girl with a lantern, and that is enough.
- **Dialogue style:** Dry humor undercuts melodrama. Pip jokes during boss fights. Sira deadpans. Toren over-explains. Liora is quiet until she isn''t.

## 8. Names — quick glossary

- **Saevora** — the world
- **First Choir** — the seven divine voices that sang the world
- **The Refrain / First Refrain** — the world-song; broken into seven Verses
- **The Hush** — silver-grey unmaking, the spreading silence
- **The Unisonist / Vaeran** — the antagonist, the cast-out Eighth Voice
- **Hushwalkers** — humans who mark Hush boundaries (Liora''s mother''s order)
- **Hushlamps** — seven silver lanterns made by the First Choir; Liora carries one
- **Verses** — learned spells / sung magic
- **Voice-tone** — a person''s unique soul-resonance
- **Vellanthe** — the cathedral city
- **The Verdigris** — the green forest country
- **Threnfall** — Liora''s home village (vertical slice location)
- **Brassmouth** — the harbor city
- **The Vault of Verses** — repository of song-magic in Vellanthe

## 9. Phase 1 vertical-slice cast (what ships first)

- **Playable:** Liora only.
- **Town NPCs in Threnfall:** Innkeeper Bram, shopkeep Mira, fisherman Old Garrick, kid named Tomi, Hushwalker Halden (mother''s old friend), grieving widow Tess, refugee child Lin, lookout Vester, priest Caleb, and "the Hushed" — a man who only remembers his name on alternate Tuesdays.
- **Dungeon:** The Silent Chapel — three rooms (vestibule, nave, sanctum) + boss room (the bell tower).
- **Enemies in the dungeon:** Hush Echo (small), Pewter Wraith, Belltongue Slime.
- **Boss:** The Hush Echo (Large) — a silver wraith with the signature ability "Silver Silence" (locks Liora out of Verse for one turn).
- **End-of-slice cinematic:** Liora exits the chapel, lantern lit, looks back at the village, and the Hush patch over the chapel *retreats one step*. Title card: *Aethersong — End of Chapter 1.*
